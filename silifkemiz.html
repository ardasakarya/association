Greenwich

Where time begins and maritime history comes alive in London's most prestigious borough

District Overview

Greenwich stands as one of London's most historically significant and culturally rich districts, renowned worldwide as the home of Greenwich Mean Time and the Prime Meridian. This UNESCO World Heritage Site seamlessly blends maritime heritage with royal history, creating an unparalleled destination that attracts millions of visitors annually.

Located in Southeast London along the Thames, Greenwich offers a unique combination of historic architecture, world-class museums, expansive parklands, and vibrant community life. From the iconic Cutty Sark to the Royal Observatory, every corner tells a story of Britain's naval supremacy and scientific advancement.

Today, Greenwich continues to thrive as both a residential haven and tourist destination, maintaining its historic character while embracing modern amenities and sustainable development practices.

Historical Timeline

Discover the rich history that shaped Greenwich from a royal residence to a world heritage site

1427

Greenwich Palace Founded

Duke Humphrey of Gloucester builds Bella Court, later becoming Greenwich Palace. This riverside location becomes a favored royal residence, setting the foundation for Greenwich's royal connections.

1491-1603

Tudor Royal Residence

Greenwich Palace becomes the birthplace of Henry VIII and Elizabeth I. The palace serves as a primary royal residence during the Tudor period, hosting elaborate court ceremonies and diplomatic events.

1696

Royal Naval College Established

Christopher Wren designs the Royal Naval College on the site of the old palace. This magnificent baroque complex becomes the training ground for Britain's naval officers, cementing Greenwich's maritime legacy.

1675

Royal Observatory Founded

Charles II establishes the Royal Observatory to solve the longitude problem for navigation. This scientific institution becomes the center of astronomical research and timekeeping, eventually defining the Prime Meridian.

1884

Greenwich Mean Time Adopted

The International Meridian Conference establishes Greenwich as the Prime Meridian (0° longitude), making Greenwich Mean Time the world's time standard and putting Greenwich at the center of global timekeeping.

1997

UNESCO World Heritage Status

Maritime Greenwich receives UNESCO World Heritage designation, recognizing its outstanding universal value and exceptional ensemble of historic buildings representing British naval and maritime history.

Location & Geography

Strategically positioned on the Thames, Greenwich offers unparalleled connectivity and natural beauty

Strategic Thames Location

Greenwich sits majestically on the south bank of the River Thames, approximately 5.5 miles southeast of central London. This prime riverside location has been crucial to its development as a maritime center and royal residence throughout history.
